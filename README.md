# My Roadmap

> A living roadmap of my journey as a software engineer.

## Mission

This repository is a living roadmap of my growth as a software engineer.

Its purpose is to document what I learn, the engineering decisions I make, the mistakes I encounter, and how my technical thinking evolves over time.

Rather than collecting notes, this repository aims to build engineering judgment through continuous learning, practical implementation, and reflection.

My long-term goal is to become an engineer capable of creating meaningful impact through technology.

Because I believe engineering is the art of making good decisions under constraints, not just writing code.

---

# Why?

Technology changes every year.

Engineering principles last for decades.

This repository focuses on understanding principles rather than memorizing tools.

The objective is to build knowledge that remains valuable regardless of programming language, framework, or company.

---

# Vision

By the age of 30, I want to be recognized as an engineer who:

- Solves complex engineering problems.
- Makes high-impact technical decisions.
- Designs reliable and scalable distributed systems.
- Influences technical direction through architecture and engineering excellence.
- Connects technology with business outcomes.

---
# Guiding Principles

These principles guide every technical decision documented in this repository.

1. Understand the problem before choosing a solution.

2. Optimize only after measuring.

3. Prefer simplicity over cleverness.

4. Every architecture is a trade-off.

5. Technology serves the business.

6. Good engineering is iterative.

7. Learn by building.

8. Document important decisions.

9. Continuously revisit previous assumptions.

10. Stay curious.
---

# Engineering Philosophy

I believe great engineers are not defined by the number of programming languages they know.

They are defined by their ability to answer questions like:

- Why this architecture?
- What problem are we solving?
- What are the trade-offs?
- How does this scale?
- What happens when it fails?
- How do we know it works?
- Is this the simplest solution that solves the problem?
- What assumptions am I making?

This repository exists to improve my ability to answer those questions.

---

# Learning Principles

Every topic follows the same principles.

## Understand the problem

Before learning a technology, understand the engineering problem it solves.

## Learn the fundamentals

Study the underlying concepts before implementation.

## Build

Apply the concept in a real project.

## Measure

Measure the impact of every important decision whenever possible.

## Document

Write the reasoning, architecture and trade-offs.

## Review

Reflect on what worked, what failed and what can be improved.

# Quarterly Review

Every three months I perform a retrospective.

Questions include:

- What did I learn?
- What changed the way I think?
- Which technical decision had the greatest impact?
- What mistakes did I make?
- What should I study next?
- Which assumption did I challenge this quarter?

Continuous improvement is more important than speed.

# Long-Term Goal

The objective is not to chase technologies or certifications.
